Why does Blockchain matter?

By Akhil Karra, November 27th 2018

Why is everyone talking about Blockchain? What is the big deal with it? Why does it matter?

The number of practical applications in which it can be used to revolutionize our lives are tremendous. Blockchain can be used for anything that requires storage and a record of any data. And as put by Professor Catherine Tucker at the MIT Sloan School of Management, "I think we can all agree that the way we store and record data has not transformed in the same way that other aspects of our use of digital data has. It makes a lot of sense to identify better technologies for recording data and ensuring its integrity." Already, blockchain is being used to track cargo shipments, manage patient records in hospitals, and find the origin of food (Garcia). It is also being explored that blockchain can be used to prevent voter fraud and to support the poor by allowing them money through blockchain ("What is Blockchain Used for Besides Bitcoin?").

Many large companies are delving into the technology of blockchain. IBM, for example, currently has over 500 projects using blockchain to solve problems in freight, banking, and food safety. One of its projects aimed at shipping freight involves the idea of cutting down the amount of paper used as much as possible to significantly reduce the human error aspect. Walmart has said that it would commit to making sure that the food they sell is safe by creating a blockchain tracing the entire path of the food, from the source to the store. American Express has experimented with blockchain for its rewards programs on credit cards. And Columbia University has expressed that it plans to launch a research center for Blockchain soon (Garcia). These are just some of the many large companies that are experimenting with Blockchain, and there may be many more in the years to come.
